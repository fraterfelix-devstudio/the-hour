# The Hour — Privacy Policy

**Version 1.0 — Last updated: June 19, 2026**
**Applies to: The Hour v1.x (offline)**

The Hour collects no personal data. There is no account to create, no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, no crash reporter, and no server for the app to talk to. The app runs entirely offline on your device.

## What the app asks for, and why

The only permission The Hour requests is **location**. It is used solely to compute and display current astrological conditions for your area. Location data is processed on your device and is never transmitted, logged, or shared. If you prefer to keep location off, the app will accept a **US ZIP code** instead — used the same way, on-device, and not transmitted.

## What we do not do

We do not collect, store, sell, rent, share, or broker any information about you. We do not build profiles. We do not track you across apps or sessions. We have no intention of monetizing user data.

## Children

Because the app collects nothing, it collects nothing from children either.

## Our commitment, now and in any future version

Even if a future version of The Hour enables online functionality, the following commitments are permanent:

- **Service-only use.** Any information collected will be used for the sole purpose of providing the in-app service the user is actively using. Not for marketing, not for profiling, not for resale.
- **We will never sell your data.** Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to anyone. There is no business model in which your data becomes someone else's product.
- **Minimum retention.** Any data we do collect will be kept only as long as required to deliver the functionality you're using, and then deleted.
- **No silent change.** A version that collects data will ship with an updated policy *before* that version goes live, the Play Store **Data safety** disclosure will be updated to match, and existing users will see an in-app notice describing what changed before any new data is collected.
- **The v1 promise is permanent.** This v1.x policy ("we collect nothing") will not be retroactively rewritten. A future version with online features will publish its own policy under its own version number; this v1.x policy will remain available as an archived record of what was promised to users of the offline app.
